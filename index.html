Weather Prediction - Hidden Markov Model

Given the observed sequence and some known probabilities, we wish to find the most likely path of the Markov chain's states. For this problem, given an observed sequence of a man bringing his umbrella on day t, we want to find the most likely sequence of states (weathers) from day 1 (t = 0) to day 5.

To solve this problem, we need additional information about the probabilities of the man bringing umbrella given the weather. For example, there could be a 0.9 chance of the guy bringing his umbrella if it is rainy, and 0.2 chance on a sunny day. We also need to know delta 0 as a starting point, which is the likelihood of most likely path leading to that point. In addition, we would also need the transition probability matrix, which is a matrix that contains the probabilities of going from one state to another (if yesterday was rainy, today might have a 50% chance to be rainy).

The algorithm I use here is Viterbi's algorithm, a technique for finding the most likely sequence of hidden states. You can read more about the algorithm here.
